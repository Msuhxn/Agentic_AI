AI tools can support writing by helping students generate ideas, organize content, and complete tasks more efficiently. For example, they may assist with drafting an outline or suggesting alternative phrasing, which can save time during the writing process. However, the output produced by these tools is not always accurate, complete, or appropriate for the intended context. AI-generated text may contain factual errors, weak argumentation, or stylistic problems that require careful revision.

Therefore, AI tools should be treated as assistants rather than replacements for human judgment. Students need to review and edit the content critically to ensure clarity, correctness, and academic quality. In this way, AI can be a useful writing aid, but its value depends on responsible and thoughtful use.
